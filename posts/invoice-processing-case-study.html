How One Firm Cut Invoice Processing Time by 85%

January 25, 2026 · 6 min read

Note: Client details have been anonymized, but the numbers and process are real.

A 12-person accounting firm in Central Florida was drowning in invoices. What started as a manageable task had grown into a monster: 15+ hours per week of pure data entry, spread across three staff members.

Three weeks after implementing automation, that number dropped to 2 hours.

The Problem

The firm managed bookkeeping for 40+ small business clients. Each month, they processed roughly 800 vendor invoices across all clients.

The old workflow looked like this:

  • Invoices arrived via email (60%), mail (30%), and client uploads (10%)
  • Staff manually opened each invoice
  • Staff typed vendor name, amount, date, and line items into QuickBooks
  • Staff assigned GL codes based on vendor/client
  • Staff filed the original document in the correct client folder

Each invoice took 3-5 minutes on average. Multiply by 800 invoices, and you're looking at 40-65 hours per month—just on data entry.

The staff hated it. Turnover was becoming a problem. And the partners knew they were paying professional rates for clerical work.

The Solution

We implemented a three-part automation system:

1. Centralized Intake: All invoices now flow to a single email address, regardless of source. Clients forward emails; mail gets scanned.

2. AI Extraction: When an invoice arrives, AI reads it and extracts: vendor name, invoice number, date, total amount, line items, and suggested GL codes based on historical patterns.

3. Human Review Queue: Extracted data appears in a review queue. Staff verify accuracy (usually a 10-second glance) and approve. Approved invoices flow directly into QuickBooks with one click.

The Implementation Timeline

Week 1: Setup & Configuration

Connected email intake, configured QuickBooks integration, trained AI on 50 sample invoices from each major vendor.

Week 2: Pilot Testing

Ran the system in parallel with manual entry. Caught edge cases, refined GL code mappings, built confidence.

Week 3: Full Rollout

Switched to automated workflow for all clients. Manual entry stopped. Staff moved to review-only mode.

The ROI Breakdown

Let's do the math:

  • Time saved: 52 hours/month × $35/hour (loaded cost) = $1,820/month
  • Automation cost: ~$500/month (tools + maintenance)
  • Net savings: $1,320/month
  • Implementation cost: ~$3,000 (one-time setup)
  • Payback period: 6 weeks

But the real value wasn't just the dollars saved. It was the staff hours freed up for higher-value work. Those 52 hours per month are now spent on client advisory, process improvement, and professional development.

Lessons Learned

1. Start with your messiest, highest-volume workflow. That's where automation delivers the biggest impact.

2. Don't aim for 100% automation. This system handles ~92% of invoices with zero human input. The other 8% need a quick review. That's fine—it's still a massive improvement.

3. Invest in training the AI. We spent several hours teaching the system this firm's specific vendors and GL mappings. That upfront investment pays dividends in accuracy.

4. Run parallel processing first. The pilot week built confidence. By the time we switched over fully, everyone trusted the system.

5 Accounting Workflows You Should Automate First (And Why)

January 28, 2026 ¬∑ 7 min read

Not all automation is created equal.

Some projects take months to implement and deliver marginal returns. Others can be set up in days and save your team 10+ hours per week immediately.

After helping dozens of firms implement automation, we've identified a clear pattern: certain workflows consistently deliver the biggest bang for your buck. Here are the top five, ranked by impact and ease of implementation.

1. üìß Invoice Processing & Data Entry

Typical time savings: 10-15 hours/week

This is the low-hanging fruit. If you're still manually typing invoice data into your accounting system, you're leaving money on the table.

What automation looks like: Invoices arrive via email. AI extracts vendor, amount, date, line items, and GL codes. Data flows directly into your accounting system. A human reviews exceptions only.

Why it works: Invoices are structured documents. AI can read them with 95%+ accuracy. The remaining 5% get flagged for review‚Äîwhich is still faster than processing everything manually.

2. üè¶ Bank Reconciliation

Typical time savings: 5-8 hours/week

Month-end reconciliation shouldn't take days. Modern tools can match 90%+ of transactions automatically.

What automation looks like: Bank feeds connect directly to your system. AI matches transactions to invoices, receipts, and journal entries. Unmatched items get flagged with suggested matches.

Why it works: Most transactions follow patterns. Once the system learns your clients' patterns, matching becomes trivial. You only spend time on genuine anomalies.

3. üìä Report Generation

Typical time savings: 3-5 hours/week

How much time do you spend pulling the same reports every month? Formatting the same spreadsheets? Copying data between systems?

What automation looks like: Reports generate automatically on a schedule. Data pulls from multiple sources. Formatting is consistent. Reports land in clients' inboxes without you lifting a finger.

Why it works: Reports are deterministic. Same inputs, same outputs. There's no reason for a human to manually run a P&L every month when a script can do it perfectly every time.

4. üìÖ Client Follow-ups & Reminders

Typical time savings: 2-4 hours/week

Chasing clients for documents is tedious, but necessary. Why do it manually?

What automation looks like: System tracks which documents are outstanding. Automatic reminders go out on a schedule. Escalation emails trigger if items stay open. You only step in when automation fails.

Why it works: Follow-ups are time-based and rules-based. If [document X] is not received by [date Y], send [reminder Z]. A computer can enforce this logic more reliably than a busy human.

5. üìÅ Document Organization & Filing

Typical time savings: 2-3 hours/week

Every document that arrives needs to be named, tagged, and filed correctly. It's mindless work‚Äîperfect for AI.

What automation looks like: Documents arrive via email or upload. AI reads them, identifies the type (invoice, receipt, contract, etc.), extracts metadata, and files them in the correct folder with the correct naming convention.

Why it works: Document classification is a solved problem. AI can identify document types with near-perfect accuracy, and filing rules are easy to define.

How to Prioritize

If you're just getting started with automation, here's our recommended order:

  1. Start with invoice processing. It's high-volume, high-impact, and the tools are mature.
  2. Add bank reconciliation. Pairs naturally with invoice processing.
  3. Automate client reminders. Quick win that improves client relationships.
  4. Tackle report generation. Frees up time for analysis instead of formatting.
  5. Implement document filing. Compounds benefits over time as your digital filing becomes immaculate.

The 80/20 Rule of Automation

Here's a secret: you don't need to automate everything perfectly. In fact, trying to achieve 100% automation often leads to diminishing returns.

The goal is to automate the predictable 80% and build efficient processes for the exceptional 20%. A system that handles 80% of invoices automatically‚Äîand flags the other 20% for quick human review‚Äîis vastly better than a system that tries to handle 100% and fails on edge cases.

Don't let perfect be the enemy of good. Start with one workflow, get it working, measure the results, and expand from there.
